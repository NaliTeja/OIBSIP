Indira Gandhi

“You cannot shake hands with a clenched fist.”

Biography

Indira Gandhi was born on November 19, 1917, in Allahabad, India. She was the daughter of Jawaharlal Nehru, India’s first Prime Minister. Her upbringing in a political family shaped her vision and commitment to public service from a young age.

Political Background

Indira was an influential member of the Indian National Congress. She became India’s first female Prime Minister in 1966. Her leadership style was known for being bold, and she played a pivotal role in shaping India’s political future during her tenure.

Major Achievements

  • Victory in the 1971 Indo-Pak war leading to Bangladesh's creation.
  • Launched the Green Revolution to boost agricultural productivity.
  • Introduced nationalization of banks and industries.
  • Authorized India’s first nuclear test in 1974.

Legacy

Despite controversies, Indira Gandhi remains a symbol of political determination. Her bold policies, leadership during war, and reforms have left a lasting impact on India. She was assassinated in 1984, but continues to be remembered as one of India’s strongest leaders.
